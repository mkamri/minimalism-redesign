Some ideas on how to get rid of stuff:

Konmari Method

Marie Kondo, a Japanese house organizer turned professional clutter-buster became a worldwide sensation, releasing a Netflix special and two popular books. She famously teaches to get close to each thing, hold it in your hands, and ask yourself if the item is truly useful or brings you joy. If not, you must thank it and let it go.

The Packing Party

This method was popularized by The Minimalists and boasts a successful decluttering in just a few weeks. Step one: invite a bunch of friends over and have them help you pack away all your stuff, as if you were moving. Step two: for an allotted time, only unpack things as you need or want them. Step three: Anything remaining in the boxes at the end gets let go.

New Japanese Minimalism

Just Do It. Nike’s slogan has been repurposed by Fumio Sasaki in his book, Goodbye, Things. He recommends a more extreme brand of minimalism for those who really want to dive head-first: get rid of everything you don’t use, even if you like it. To Sasaki, happiness is not having what you want, but wanting what you have.
